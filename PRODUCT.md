# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo self-employed tutor — single user, opens the app daily between lessons. Manages 5–20 students across subjects. Juggles scheduling, homework tracking, and payment collection simultaneously. Fluent in Russian; operates entirely on desktop.

## Product Purpose

TutorDesk is an all-in-one private dashboard for an independent tutor: schedule, student profiles (with homework and progress), payments, and finances in one place. Success looks like: the tutor spends zero mental energy on admin and zero minutes copying between spreadsheets and calendars. The app also has a separate public landing page that markets TutorDesk to other tutors considering adopting it.

## Brand Personality

Polished, ambitious, precise. Feels like a premium SaaS tool built specifically for educators — not a generic dashboard clone. Confident in its opinions.

## Anti-references

- Generic Tailwind-starter-kit dashboards (flat, gray, symmetric card grids, no hierarchy)
- Cold enterprise admin panels (dense tables, zero personality, audit-log aesthetic)
- Bloated SaaS with a feature for everything (TutorDesk is for one tutor, one workflow)

## Design Principles

1. **Trust at a glance** — the tutor scans screens between lessons. Visual hierarchy must be immediate: one look = one insight. Data before decoration.
2. **Function earns delight** — micro-interactions and motion are welcome only where they confirm actions or guide attention. Never decorative for its own sake.
3. **Depth, not complexity** — a daily-use tool for one expert user. Optimize for power, not discoverability. Density is a feature.
4. **Two registers, one brand** — the landing page can breathe and sell; the app is dense and efficient. They share a visual language, not a layout pattern.
5. **No rough edges** — a tutor trusts this app with their business. Missing states, error handling, and edge cases must be designed, not left to chance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigation throughout the app shell. Minimum 4.5:1 body text contrast; 3:1 for large text. Desktop-only for MVP (no mobile breakpoints required yet). Reduced motion support for all transitions.
